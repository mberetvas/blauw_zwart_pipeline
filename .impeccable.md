## Design Context

### Users
Club Brugge supporters using the browser-based AI surfaces as a second-screen companion around matchdays. They move between chat, stats, shopping, and leaderboard views to check status quickly, compare themselves with other fans, and feel part of a premium club community on both mobile and desktop.

### Brand Personality
Electric, editorial, and assured. The interface should feel like a night match at Jan Breydel: atmospheric, competitive, and premium rather than playful or casual.

### Aesthetic Direction
The product direction is **Stadium Nocturne**: dark-first surfaces, tonal layering instead of hard dividers, sharp editorial typography, and disciplined Club Brugge blue accents. Floating moments can use glass and glow sparingly, but the overall feel should stay refined, high-contrast, and data-forward.

### Design Principles
1. Use tonal depth, not rigid lines, to separate layers and hierarchy.
2. Make key states feel alive through motion that suggests momentum, rank, and progression.
3. Keep the experience premium and legible: bold headlines, restrained accents, no noisy gamification clutter.
4. Optimize for lightweight browser performance on mid-range mobile and desktop hardware: transform and opacity first, minimal JavaScript, no layout-thrashing animation.
5. Respect accessibility by supporting reduced motion and preserving clear contrast in every interactive state.
